# STARWAKE PROTOCOL — LORE BIBLE

**Canonical narrative reference — living document**

This file is maintained as the narrative source of truth for Starwake Protocol. Brandon and ChatGPT develop the world together; ChatGPT is responsible for maintaining continuity, proposing narrative structure, and ensuring lore serves the game rather than decorating it.

## Narrative Rules

1. Every piece of lore should explain, contextualize, or reinforce a mechanic.
2. Every faction must justify its gameplay identity.
3. Every boss must exist for a reason.
4. Every soundtrack must belong to a place, state, faction, or dramatic function.
5. Every enemy must have a purpose beyond being something to shoot.
6. Lore should be discoverable rather than forced into active play.
7. Mystery is useful only when the underlying answer is internally coherent.
8. Canonical facts, in-world beliefs, and deliberate misinformation must be labeled separately.
9. Names and terminology should remain consistent across code, interface, dialogue, and documentation.
10. New canon should not be finalized until its mechanical purpose is understood.

## Current Canon Status

The full narrative is intentionally not locked yet. Existing working concepts—including the Starwake Network, the Protocol, anomalous units, the player’s relationship to iteration, and the meaning of repeated runs—remain provisional until reconciled with the current game systems.

## Open Narrative Questions

- What exactly is the Starwake?
- Who or what authorized the Protocol?
- Is the player a pilot, an AI, a human–machine composite, or something that changes over time?
- Why do runs repeat, and how much does the world remember?
- Why do bosses recognize or anticipate the player?
- Which enemy families are factions, functions, corrupted remnants, or deliberate defenses?
- What does research represent inside the world?
- What can be communicated through enemy behavior and sound before any text explains it?
